The function of the ON and WHERE clauses in the various types of the joins like INNER, OUTER, LEFT, RIGHT and more are might be a bit confusing. The following facts may help you keep things straight:


  1. The ON clause is part of the INNER, LEFT, RIGHT, and FULL joins. The CROSS join and UNION join don't have an ON clause because neither of them does any filtering of the data.


  2. The ON clause in an inner join is logically equivalent to a WHERE clause. i.e. the same condition could be specified either in an ON clause or a WHERE clause


  3. The ON clauses in outer join i.e. LEFT, RIGHT, and FULL joins are different from WHERE clauses.


    1. The WHERE clause simply filter the rows returned by the FROM clause. Rows rejected by the filter are not included in the result.

    2. The ON clause in an outer join first filters the rows of a cross product and then includes the rejected rows, extended with nulls values.
